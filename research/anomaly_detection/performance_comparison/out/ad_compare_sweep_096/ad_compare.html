Anomaly Detector Comparison

What this report shows

This compares anomaly detectors on the selected dataset. Training uses baseline windows (train* labels). Evaluation uses all other windows (baselines not marked train + faults). Thresholds are calibrated on train scores at a fixed quantile; metrics (precision/recall/F1) are at that operating point, and PR/ROC AUCs are computed from continuous scores.

Threshold quantile

THRESHOLD_QUANTILE = 0.9600. We set the anomaly threshold so that this fraction of baseline (train) scores fall below it. Lowering it (e.g., 0.980 → 0.970) typically increases recall at the cost of precision.

Metric definitions

  • Precision: TP / (TP + FP) — of predicted anomalies, how many are true faults.
  • Recall: TP / (TP + FN) — of true faults, how many were detected.
  • F1: harmonic mean of precision and recall — balances both.
  • PR-AUC: area under Precision–Recall curve — ranking quality for the positive class.
  • ROC-AUC: area under ROC curve — overall separability between classes.

Detectors in this report

  • IsolationForest (sklearn): tree-based isolation; higher score ⇒ more anomalous.
  • Extended IsolationForest (isotree): enhanced IF (if installed); higher score ⇒ more anomalous.
  • COPOD (pyod): copula-based outlier degree; higher score ⇒ more anomalous.
  • RRCF (rrcf): random cut forest; CoDisp score; higher score ⇒ more anomalous.
